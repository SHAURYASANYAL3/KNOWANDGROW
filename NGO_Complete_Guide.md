## **1. NGO Core Activities & Best Practices**

### **A. Mission & Strategic Planning**
**What to Do:**
1. **Define Clear Mission Statement**
   - 1-2 sentence statement of purpose
   - Aligned with community needs and global goals (UN SDGs)
   - Example: "Empowering youth through mental health awareness and digital safety education"

2. **Create Theory of Change**
   - Map inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact
   - Include measurable indicators for each stage

3. **Conduct Needs Assessment**
   - Surveys, focus groups, community consultations
   - Partner with local organizations for ground-level insights

**Best Practices:**
- Involve beneficiaries in planning process
- Use data-driven decision making
- Regularly update strategic plans (annual review)

### **B. Funding & Resource Management**
**What to Do:**
1. **Diversify Funding Sources**
   - **Grants**: Government schemes, international foundations
   - **Donations**: Individual donors, corporate CSR
   - **Earned Income**: Training fees, resource sales
   - **Crowdfunding**: Milaap, Ketto, GoFundMe

2. **Financial Management**
   - Maintain separate bank account for NGO
   - Implement double-entry accounting
   - Regular financial reporting (monthly/quarterly)

3. **Resource Mobilization**
   - Build relationships with donors
   - Create compelling proposals
   - Track donor requirements and reporting

**Best Practices:**
- Maintain 6-month operational reserve
- Use accounting software (Tally, QuickBooks)
- Regular audits (internal/external)

### **C. Program Implementation**
**What to Do:**
1. **Design Evidence-Based Programs**
   - Use proven methodologies
   - Include monitoring & evaluation frameworks
   - Build in flexibility for local adaptation

2. **Community Engagement**
   - Train local volunteers as program ambassadors
   - Create feedback mechanisms
   - Build community ownership

3. **Partnership Development**
   - Government agencies (health, education departments)
   - Other NGOs (complementary services)
   - Corporate partners (CSR initiatives)
   - Academic institutions (research support)

**Best Practices:**
- Start with pilot programs
- Document lessons learned
- Scale successful models

### **D. Advocacy & Awareness**
**What to Do:**
1. **Policy Advocacy**
   - Research policy gaps
   - Build evidence-based recommendations
   - Engage with policymakers
   - Use media strategically

2. **Public Awareness**
   - Social media campaigns
   - Community events and workshops
   - Media partnerships (TV, radio, print)
   - Influencer collaborations

3. **Capacity Building**
   - Train community members
   - Build volunteer networks
   - Create resource materials

**Best Practices:**
- Use storytelling effectively
- Build coalitions for stronger advocacy
- Measure awareness impact

### **E. Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning**
**What to Do:**
1. **Develop M&E Framework**
   - Define indicators (output, outcome, impact)
   - Set baseline data
   - Plan data collection methods
   - Schedule reporting timelines

2. **Data Collection & Analysis**
   - Use surveys, interviews, focus groups
   - Track program metrics
   - Analyze qualitative and quantitative data

3. **Learning & Adaptation**
   - Regular review meetings
   - Share findings with stakeholders
   - Adapt programs based on evidence

**Best Practices:**
- Use simple, practical tools
- Build M&E into program design
- Share results transparently

## **2. Website Requirements for an NGO**

### **A. Essential Pages & Features**

#### **1. Homepage**
**Must-Have Elements:**
- Clear mission statement (above the fold)
- High-quality hero image/video
- Primary call-to-action (Donate, Volunteer, Get Help)
- Trust indicators (partner logos, impact statistics)
- Quick links to key resources

**Design Guidelines:**
- Clean, uncluttered layout
- Mobile-first responsive design
- Fast loading speed (<3 seconds)
- Accessible color contrast (WCAG AA standards)

#### **2. About Us Page**
**Content Requirements:**
- NGO registration details (Trust/Society/ Section 8 Company)
- FCRA registration number (if applicable)
- Board of directors/members profiles
- Organizational history and milestones
- Annual reports and financial statements

**Trust-Building Elements:**
- Photos of team and activities
- Media coverage and testimonials
- Partner organization logos
- Impact metrics and success stories

## **3. Legal Requirements for NGOs (India-Focused)**

### **A. Registration Requirements**

#### **1. Types of NGO Registrations in India**
| Registration Type | Suitable For | Key Features | Minimum Members |
---|---|---|---|
| **Trust** | Charitable activities, religious institutions | Simple registration, property management | 2 trustees |
| **Society** | Educational, cultural, charitable | Democratic management, annual meetings | 7 members |
| **Section 8 Company** | Charitable, social welfare, education | Limited liability, corporate structure | 2 directors, 2 members |

#### **2. Registration Process**
**Trust Registration:**
1. Create trust deed (stamp paper, notarized)
2. Submit to local registrar
3. Obtain PAN and TAN
4. Open bank account

**Society Registration:**
1. Draft memorandum and rules
2. File with state registrar
3. Obtain certificate of registration
4. Apply for PAN and TAN

**Section 8 Company:**
1. Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
2. Director Identification Number (DIN)
3. Name approval from ROC
4. File SPICe+ form
5. Obtain certificate of incorporation
6. Apply for PAN and TAN

#### **3. Documents Required**
- Identity proof (PAN card, Aadhaar, Voter ID)
- Address proof (electricity bill, rent agreement)
- Passport size photographs
- Bank account details
- Trust deed/memorandum & articles

### **B. Tax Compliance**

#### **1. Tax Exemptions**
**80G Certificate:**
- Allows donors to claim tax deductions
- Two types: 100% exemption (80G), 50% exemption (80G(5))
- Application to Income Tax Department
- Requires audited financial statements

**12A Registration:**
- Exempts NGO from paying income tax
- Automatic exemption for donations
- Requires application to Income Tax Department

**FCRA Registration:**
- Required for receiving foreign contributions
- Application to Ministry of Home Affairs
- Renewal every 5 years
- Stringent compliance requirements

#### **2. Tax Returns & Filings**
- **ITR-7**: For trusts and Section 8 companies
- **Audit Requirements**: Mandatory if income exceeds ₹2.5 lakhs
- **Form 10**: For claiming 80G exemption
- **Annual Returns**: Form 11 for FCRA compliance

### **C. Financial Compliance**

#### **1. Accounting Standards**
- Follow Companies Act 2013 (for Section 8 companies)
- Use accrual accounting method
- Maintain proper books of accounts
- Regular bank reconciliation

#### **2. Audit Requirements**
- **Internal Audit**: Quarterly/semi-annual
- **External Audit**: Annual (CA firm)
- **FCRA Audit**: Additional audit for foreign funds
- **Cost Audit**: If applicable

#### **3. Financial Reporting**
- **Balance Sheet**
- **Income & Expenditure Account**
- **Cash Flow Statement**
- **Notes to Accounts**
- **Annual Report** (for stakeholders)

### **D. Data Protection & Privacy**

#### **1. Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023**
**Key Requirements:**
- **Consent Management**: Explicit consent for data collection
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only necessary data
- **Data Security**: Reasonable security practices
- **Data Subject Rights**: Right to access, correct, delete data
- **Data Breach Notification**: Report breaches within 72 hours

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Create privacy policy
2. Implement consent management system
3. Train staff on data protection
4. Establish data breach response plan
5. Appoint data protection officer (if required)

#### **2. IT Act 2000 Compliance**
- **Reasonable Security Practices**: Section 43A
- **Data Protection**: Section 72A
- **Cyber Café Guidelines**: If applicable
- **Intermediary Guidelines**: For online platforms

### **E. Online & Content Compliance**

#### **1. Content Regulations**
- **Avoid Hate Speech**: Section 153A of IPC
- **Defamation Laws**: Section 499 of IPC
- **Copyright Compliance**: Use original or licensed content
- **Trademark Protection**: Protect NGO name/logo
- **Child Protection**: If working with minors, comply with POCSO Act

#### **2. Website Compliance**
- **Accessibility**: WCAG 2.1 AA standards
- **Terms of Use**: Clear terms for website usage
- **Disclaimer**: For advice and information provided
- **Cookie Policy**: If using tracking cookies
- **Accessibility Statement**: Commitment to accessibility

#### **3. Online Fundraising Compliance**
- **Payment Gateway Regulations**: RBI guidelines
- **Fund Utilization Reporting**: Regular updates to donors
- **Refund Policy**: Clear refund terms
- **Foreign Contribution**: FCRA compliance for international donations

### **F. Employment & Labor Laws**

#### **1. Key Labor Laws**
- **Shops & Establishments Act**: State-specific regulations
- **Payment of Wages Act**: Timely salary payments
- **Employees' Provident Fund**: For eligible employees
- **Employees' State Insurance**: Health insurance
- **Professional Tax**: State-level tax

#### **2. Volunteer Management**
- **Volunteer Agreement**: Clear terms of engagement
- **Insurance Coverage**: Accident insurance for volunteers
- **Training & Safety**: Proper training and safety measures
- **Recognition Programs**: Volunteer appreciation

### **G. State-Specific Requirements (India)**

#### **1. Maharashtra**
- **Maharashtra Public Trusts Act**: For trusts registered in Maharashtra
- **Shops & Establishments Act**: For office operations
- **Local Body Permissions**: For events and activities

#### **2. Delhi**
- **Delhi Societies Registration Act**: For societies
- **Delhi Shops & Establishments Act**: For office operations
- **Fire Department NOC**: For office spaces

#### **3. Karnataka**
- **Karnataka Societies Registration Act**: For societies
- **Karnataka Public Trusts Act**: For trusts
- **Local Body Permissions**: For events
